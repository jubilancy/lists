You can move your site to WordPress.com from almost any platform. This guide covers import options for sites built with WordPress and other platforms. If you know what kind of site you are importing, select the option from the table of contents in this guide.

## Import from WordPress.org

A WordPress.org site (also called self-hosted WordPress) is a WordPress site you host yourself (including sites developed locally) or with a third-party hosting provider like Bluehost, SiteGround, or GoDaddy.

**Thinking about migrating your WordPress site to WordPress.com?**

Our expert team can migrate the site for you while your live site remains unaffected—at no cost *and*  with 50% off your first year on our  [Business plan](https://wordpress.com/support/plan-features/business-plan/).

The migration process typically takes 2–3 business days. Once it’s complete, we’ll guide you through connecting your domain and getting your new site up and running. To get started, enter your site details and pick the “Migrate site” option during the migration setup below. We’ll handle the rest.

[Request a Free Migration](https://wordpress.com/support/request-a-free-migration/)

Learn more about the [migration process](https://wordpress.com/support/request-a-free-migration/#what-to-expect).

Or, use one of the following methods to import your site to WordPress.com:

- [Self-service migration](https://wordpress.com/support/import/import-an-entire-wordpress-site/): Best for most users who want a hands-off experience. Imports content, media, plugins, and themes.
- [All-in-One WP Migration plugin](https://wordpress.com/support/import/import-using-all-in-one-migration/): Best for users familiar with plugins who want more control. Imports content, media, plugins, and themes.

The above options require the purchase of a [WordPress.com Business plan or higher](https://wordpress.com/plans/) so that plugins can be imported.

Free sites and sites on any paid plan can use the [content-only import](https://wordpress.com/support/import/import-a-sites-content/) option to import pages, posts, and media.

## Import from another WordPress.com site

If you already have a site on WordPress.com, choose the option that fits your needs:

- [Transfer the site](https://wordpress.com/support/transfer-a-site/): Move your entire site to a different WordPress.com account.
- [Copy a site](https://wordpress.com/support/copy-a-site/): Create a duplicate for testing or a new project.
- [Self-service migration](https://wordpress.com/support/import/import-an-entire-wordpress-site/): Import content, media, plugins, and themes to another site.
- [Content-only import](https://wordpress.com/support/import/import-a-sites-content/): Move just pages, posts, and media to another site.
- [Change the address](https://wordpress.com/support/changing-site-address/): Update your site’s URL without moving content.

You can import your site to WordPress.com even if it wasn’t built on WordPress. Select your current platform below.

### Other platforms

If your platform isn’t listed above, see [converting an HTML site to WordPress](https://wordpress.com/support/import/converting-an-html-site-to-wordpress/) for instructions on using standard export files.

## Import from WordPress Playground

[WordPress Playground](https://wordpress.org/playground/) is a tool that runs WordPress directly in your browser—no server required. It’s useful for learning, testing, and development.

You can import Playground projects to WordPress.com using XML export files, ZIP exports, or Playground ZIP files. [Import to a new site here.](https://wordpress.com/setup/migration-signup)
