### **Streaming Services**
* **Tidal:** High-fidelity streaming known for better artist payouts.
* **Spotify:** The most common mainstream streaming app.
* **Apple Music:** Apple's native streaming service.
* **YouTube / YouTube Music:** Google’s video and music streaming platforms.
* **SoundCloud:** Known for hosting user-uploaded tracks, remixes, and unreleased music.
* **Audiomack:** A streaming platform popular for mixtapes and independent artists.
* **Bandcamp:** A platform where fans can buy music directly from artists (digital and physical).
* **Demus:** An iOS app that allows users to stream music (often sourced from YouTube) and organize a library without a subscription.

### **Media Players & Software**
* **VLC Media Player:** A versatile, open-source player that handles almost any file format.
* **foobar2000 (mobile):** A highly customizable advanced audio player.
* **Poweramp:** A popular Android-only local music player with a powerful audio engine.
* **Vox:** A high-res music player for Mac and iOS, often used for FLAC files.
* **Lark Player:** An Android music and video player often used for offline files.
* **Musi:** An iOS app that streams music from YouTube (sometimes faces App Store availability issues).
* **iTunes:** Apple’s legacy software for managing local music libraries and syncing devices.
* **Rockbox:** Open-source custom firmware used to replace the original OS on MP3 players (like iPods) to add more features and format support.

### **Download & Piracy Tools**
* **YouTube Revanced / YouTube Music Revanced:** Patched versions of the official apps that provide Premium features (like no ads) for free.
* **yt-dlp:** A command-line tool used to download videos/audio from YouTube and other sites.
* **Soulseek:** A peer-to-peer (P2P) file-sharing network famous for high-quality and obscure music finds.
* **Internet Archive:** A digital library that hosts a massive amount of free, legal music and live recordings.
* **ytmp3:** A generic term for web-based "YouTube to MP3" converters.

### **Hardware & Formats**
* **iPod Classic:** The iconic Apple dedicated music player.
* **MP3 Player:** General term for dedicated digital audio players (DAPs).
* **Vinyls & CDs:** Physical music formats.
* **FLAC / MP3 / Mpeg audio layer iii:** Digital audio file formats (FLAC is lossless; MP3 is compressed).
