# **ENGLISH AND ITS VARIATIONS**
- [Oxford English Dictionary](http://oed.com/) The best there is, but not free.↳
- [r/etymology](https://www.reddit.com/r/etymology/) Currently the most useful place to begin.
- [English Language & Usage/Stack Exchange](https://english.stackexchange.com/) A question-and-answer site "for linguists, etymologists, and serious English language enthusiasts." I get more useful information here on a daily basis than anywhere else.↳
- [Old English Translator](https://www.oldenglishtranslator.co.uk/) Works both ways (Old English to Modern, Modern English to Old).↳
- [Bosworth Toller's Anglo-Saxon Dictionary online](http://www.bosworthtoller.com/), up to the 1921 supplement, hosted by the Faculty of Arts, Charles University in Prague. Free and searchable.
- [Online version of the University of Michigan Middle English Dictionary](https://quod.lib.umich.edu/m/med/) "\- [D\]escribed as 'the greatest achievement in medieval scholarship in America.' Its 15,000 pages offer a comprehensive analysis of lexicon and usage for the period 1100-1500, based on the analysis of a collection of over three million citation slips, the largest collection of this kind available."↳
- [Dictionaries of the Scots Language](https://dsl.ac.uk/) "Scotland’s independent lexicographical body for the Scots language."
- [English Synonyms Explained and Illustrated](https://books.google.com/books?id=g2MLAAAAMAAJ&printsec=frontcover#v=onepage&q&f=false) By J.H.A. Günther.
- [Languagehat](http://www.languagehat.com/) A fine blog by a linguist whose interest reaches beyond the words. The "links" bar is one of the best for language resources.↳
- [Omniglot](http://www.omniglot.com/blog/) Language-related musings by Welsh blogger and linguist Simon Ager.
- [Things to be Happy About](http://www.thingstobehappyabout.com/) Website of linguist Barbara Ann Kipfer, who edited the most recent edition of the "Dictionary of American Slang."
- [The American Heritage Dictionary with Indo-European Roots Appendix](https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html) The latest iteration of the Calvert Watkins list.↳
# **HISTORY**
- [Talia Felix's blog of history and folklore](https://https://blog.hoodoo-online.com/) Embark on a mystical journey filled with tales of wonder, supernatural beings, and age-old wisdom. Highlights include - [Antoine Wiertz's Severed Head Experiment](https://spellcasters-source.blogspot.com/2019/09/visions-of-guillotined-head.html), - [Four Thieves Vinegar History](https://spellcasters-source.blogspot.com/2010/06/four-thieves-vinegar.html), - [Mary King's Close](https://blog.hoodoo-online.com/2012/08/mary-king-close-v-origin.html), and - [a Quick History of Zombies](https://spellcasters-source.blogspot.com/2023/11/shocking-secrets-of-mysterious-zombies.html).↳
- [Quote Investigator](https://quoteinvestigator.com/) This website records the investigations by Garson O’Toole into well-known quotations.
- [Work in food history](https://anthonybuccini.com/work-in-food-history/) "Historical linguistics, dialectology, sociolinguistics, and food history" by Anthony F. Buccini, Ph.D.
- [Early American Cookery](https://www.youtube.com/@EarlyAmerican/videos) Historical recipes cooked with historical tools and techniques, made into little films you can watch.
- [Roman Inscriptions of Britain](http://romaninscriptionsofbritain.org) Need to kill some time? Browse this collection of Roman inscriptions discovered around the UK.
- [A glossary and etymological dictionary of obsolete and uncommon words](https://books.google.com/books?id=s3cSAAAAIAAJ&printsec=frontcover&dq=William+Toone+Etymological+Dictionary+of+Obsolete+Words&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwjeqcj6xsjWAhWVF8AKHfOtBxgQ6AEILTAB#v=onepage&q&f=false) By William Toone.
- [David Rumsey Historical Map Collection](https://www.davidrumsey.com/) Old time maps from around the world.
- [History Learning Site](http://www.historylearningsite.co.uk) Does what it says on the can.
- [Digital Medievalist](http://digitalmedievalist.com/) Celtic Studies Resources from a self-described "opinionated digital medievalist."
# **NON-ENGLISH DICTIONARIES AND ETYMOLOGY**
- [Geiriadur Prifysgol Cymru](http://www.welsh-dictionary.ac.uk/) The standard historical dictionary of the Welsh language. The vocabulary is defined in Welsh, and English equivalents are also given. Detailed attention is given to variant forms, collocations, and etymology.↳
- [The Anglo-Norman Online Hub.](http://www.anglo-norman.net/)
- [Chinese Characters](http://zhongwen.com/) Contains the complete text of "Chinese Characters: A Genealogy and Dictionary," which uses the new (zipu) system of character trees.
- [Chinese Etymology.](http://hanziyuan.net/)
- [An Etymological Dictionary of the French Language](https://archive.org/details/etymologicaldict00bracrich) Digitized version of Auguste Brachet's book of 1873.
- [Italian Etymology](http://www.etimo.it/) A searchable online version of the 1907 "Vocabolario Etimologico della Lingua Italiana" by Ottorino Pianigiani.
- [English-Persian Etymological Dictionary of Astronomy and Astrophysics](http://aramis2.obspm.fr/~heydari/dictionary/) "Aims at contributing to the Persian language by creating a comprehensive dictionary of astronomy and astrophysics."↳
- [The German etymological dictionary of the Brothers Grimm](http://www.dwb.uni-trier.de/) Das Deutsche Wörterbuch von Jacob und Wilhelm Grimm auf CD-ROM und im Internet.
- [Another German etymological dictionary](https://www.dwds.de/), Digitales Wörterbuch der deutschen Sprache.
- [Historische woordenboeken Nederlands en Fries](http://gtb.inl.nl/search/) (Dutch). 
- [Sumerian Language Page](http://www.sumerian.org/)
- [Latdict](http://latin-dictionary.net/) Kevin Mahoney's online Latin dictionary and grammar resource.
- [Logeion](https://logeion.uchicago.edu/about) Latin and Greek dictionary which includes hard to find medieval vocabulary.
- [Dickinson College Commentaries](https://dcc.dickinson.edu/) Latin and Greek texts for reading, with explanatory notes, essays, vocabulary, and graphic, video, and audio elements.
- [English Words of Arabic Ancestry](https://archive.org/download/EnglishWordsThatAreOfArabicEtymologicalAncestry/English-words-that-are-of-Arabic-etymological-ancestry.htm) Thoughtful and thoroughly researched work (archive version, scroll down to get to the text.)
- [Lexilogos](https://www.lexilogos.com/english/index.htm) (English version) A comprehensive set of dictionaries and other online resources for the study of the languages of the world.
- [Verbix](http://www.verbix.com/) Free online verb conjugator in English, French, German, Italian, Arabic, Japanese, Latin, Spanish, and other languages.
- [Indo-European Lexicon: Pokorny Master PIE Etyma](https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master) From the University of Texas Linguistics Research Center.
- [Austronesian Comparative Dictionary](https://www.trussel2.com/ACD/introduction.htm) Comparing Austronesian languages, from Madagascar to Samoa
